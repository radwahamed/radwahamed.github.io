Patina

Why do somethings get better with age?

  • Created by: Radwa Hamed
  • Completed on: Fall 2019
  • Skills: Design Thinking/ Prototyping

Project Info:

"The most sustainable product is the one you use forever and never throw away." Critiques for the design process usually highlight the fact that it by its very definition creates over-consumption, resource depletion and environmental damage. With the motivation of dismanteling the design of landfill, I worked on a team of 3 to study what motivates people to hold onto products for years.

After doing 7 interviews and building up empathy with the different interviewees, we found. that people had interesting insights about the value of products. One of the most interesting interviews was one with John Hennessy where he showed us multiple amazing products.

After analyzing the different inputs from the interviewees and organizing them into an empathy framework, we realized three main insights about how people hold onto products for three different types of pride: pride of heritage & culture, pride of recognition and pride of mastery.

We followed in with the design process with brainstorming and prototyping 3 ideas: a textile that encodes our memories in a way that can only be seen with specific lens, a shoe sole that counts are steps and makes you eligible for upgrades and finally a returnable togo box that allows people to share their eating moments with others who reuse the same togo box; it also allows them to monitor where did their togo boxxes travel and their environmental footprint.
