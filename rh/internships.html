How did my internships change my take on life?

Technical Experience

Being part of many huge organizations has not only exposed me to many technical challenges but also helped me survey the market and understand what I need to do to excel and make a difference in this industry!

Great Networking

Diversity and individuality is one of my biggest takes out of the internships. I am forever grateful for having multiple chances to meet amazing people from all over the world who get to make a change in this world as part of their daily work. This is not only confined to the great people I worked with and their unlimited innovation but also the people I met along the way outside the tech industry.

Working Under Pressure

Whether it was a research internship like Microsoft Advanced Technology Labs or Affectiva, where I had to stipulate a new way to approach the problem and synthesize a solution out of it, or a product based internship like the ones in Google where I had to be up to serving products for billions of people, working under pressure is definitely an asset I gained through my internships.

Traveling The World

I have a specific section in my portfolio dedicated to my love for travel but it is safe to state that without my internships I would have never finished 25 cities around the world before the age of 25.

Cairo, Egypt. Summer 2013
Mentor: Tamer Eldeeb and Mahmoud Osama

My project was implementing a gaze detection system to be included in Microsoft products for scrollig purposes. I did a literature survey, created a data collection tool, did face detection and landmark tracking of the eye and then did image processing and regression modeling to estimate the position of the iris and its direction of movement.

The technology for these projects were using: C++, Matlab and C#. This was the first machine learning project I engaged in.

Google SWE Intern

Analytics Eng Prod Team

Mountain View, CA, USA. Summer 2016
Mentor: Dr. Todd Jackson

I was part of Google Analytics. My project responsible for fuzz testing the analytics server. the idea was to create inputs that could break the 8-year-old, thousands-of-lines-of-code server.

For this task I took multiple approaches as guided by one of my greatest mentors: Todd Jackson. We designed the system to generate this fuzzy inputs using randomizations, mutations and genetic algorithms; the project was huge and wasn't fully completed by the end of the internship. However, we achieved very high code coverage and discovered multiple loopholes in the server along the way which was the original purpose.

Affectiva Machine Learning Intern

Speech Science Team

Cairo, Egypt. Spring 2017
Mentor: Dr.Taniya Mishra and Mohamed Ezz

Affectiva is a spin-off from MIT Media Lab founded by Rana Elkaliouby and Prof. Rosalind Picard. It is based out of Boston, Ma. It does emotion recognition from video and speech. The speech models are fairly recent with no infrastructure for the model design and data consumption as well as lack of proper data. My project responsible for creating a Deep Learning Framework for speech and using Transfer Learning to create a robust emotion model for speech..

For this task I took multiple approaches including unsupervised learning, semi-supervised learning, ensemble learning and finally transfer learning. We used pre-trained models for speech recognition and gender detection and retrained them for emotion recognition. We exceeded the baseline and continuous improvement to this framework and these models are in production as we speak.

Google SWE Intern

Knowledge Graph Recon Service Team

Zurich, Switzerland. Summer 2017
Mentor: Peter Dornbach

This is by far one of my favorite internships ever. Knowledge Graph is by far the biggest data lake I have ever dealt with. My project was responsible for creating a Flume - Map Reduce- pipeline on top of the daily traffic into the graph data in order to create summary tables for the engineers to query. My team was responsible for reconciling new data entering the graph with already existing nodes to creat relations and avoid duplicates.

I was also responsible for synthesizing dashboards with statistics about the new data entering on top of the summary tables created for the engineers to sanitize the graph and detect any anomalies.
