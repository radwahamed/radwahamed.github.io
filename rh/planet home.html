Planet Home

Winner of NYUAD Hackathon and HULT International Competition for social good

  • Created by: Aleya Farhoud, Radwa Hamed, Yassmeen Fouad
  • Skills: Social Entrepreneurship / Technology / Business Development

Project Idea:

Planet Home is the first gig-economy platform, helping vulnerable populations find secure jobs, overcome the language barrier and have access to viable payment solutions. Our end-to-end solution starts by reaching refugee communities in urban settings and assessing their skills by having a mobile office in our key cities. We provide a low-tech sms solution for refugees, and integrate this with a high-tech webapp for local citizens. From there, we begin making matches. Refugees sign up with their skillset and are matched with citizens in need of these services. The machine translation facilitates the communication between them. The refugee is guaranteed fair payment and is given access to an e-wallet in the form of vouchers they can use in any store they select which provides them with access to better living and social integration.

This app won first place in HULT Competition in The American University in Cairo and qualified to finals in Shanghai. It also won first place in New York University Abu Dhabi Hackathon.

The app was considered for many humanitarian accelerators in UAE and was awarded many other recognitions found in the awards section. However, the app was discontinued for problems in many of the legal policies concerend with international refugee law and internet governance.

I am currently part of a campaign called "Count My Voice" that promotes sustainable internet governance and better policy making process that takes into consideration all the stakeholders and takes into consideration the shifting demographics.
