DEA creates an interactive storytelling experience that transports kids into world of imagination. Kids customize the book characters and endings and then project them on the wall in a DIY projector

The Untold

The Untold transfers Egypt into open air museum where visitors integrate directly with the touristic ecosystem using simultaneous localization and mapping to bring them to life.

The project was motivated by the need to protect uncharted cultural heritage, own the egyptian nnarrative and provide a solid way for data collection and analytics.

Hi! I am Radwa

Here'ss to the fools who dream

When I was younger, my mum used to read me bed time stories every night and ask me to give them an ending. Every night it was the same ending. my mum would always ask “Who is going to put other superheroes to sleep today? Who is going to slay their dragons?” and my answer was always as enthused: “me, mum. ME!”

I grew to know that I can't be a super hero but I can still aspire to change the world and help people slay their own dragons. I am currently pursuing my Masters in Design Impact at Stanford University. I am a practitionar of human centric design in an attempt to help create better products and services with a focus on education and maintaing cultural heritage & identity. I am part of the 2019 Knight-Hennessy Cohort at Stanford, a program working on developing a community of future global leaders to address complex challenges through collaboration & innovation.

I come from a closely-knit Egyptian family. I learnt the meaning of strength from my mum. I learnt the meaning of perseverence from my siblings. In a previous life I was a software engineer who is always involved in extra-curricular activities . I joined the American University in Cairo- something I worked diligently for. I double majored in Computer Engineering and Electronics Engineering with a tenacious lust towards learning everything about technology. I did multiple internships in many tech giants like Microsoft Research, Google, UC Berkeley and Affectiva. However, this is not how I view my story. I contain multitudes.
