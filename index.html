My Story

"Here's to the fools who dream"

"Look where I ended up dear diary; a girl from Cairo with a big heart and dreamy eyes travelling the world and trying to make the slightest impact she can." ~ My Diary Entry, November, 2018.
I am one of three siblings in a middle class Egyptian family. I lost my loving father when I was 13 and it is then that I learnt the meaning of strength from my mum. I learnt the meaning of perseverence from my siblings and I learnt how to not settle. Upon graduating from school, I joined the American University in Cairo- something I worked diligently for. I double majored in Computer Engineering and Electronics Engineering with a tenacious lust towards learning everything about technology from the transistor level to the UI level. I did a lot of internships inside Egypt and in many tech giants like Microsoft Research, Google, UC Berkeley and Affectiva. However, this is not how I see my story.
I see my story in the great friendships I made. I see my story in the endless memories I made. I see my story in every scent I smelled and every corner I visited. I see my story in the impact people have left on me and the impact I have started to do and want to continue doing! Every Light you shine on others reflects on you | أي نور تتركه داخل احدهم يضيئك. I'll continue to write a story where I can light at least one person's life and maybe it'll reflect onto the world.

My Philosophy

"Dog Days are over."

There is this very famous star called "The Dog Star", or Sirius, it used to shine so hard during time of the flood in ancient Egypt and people used to think of it as time of suffering, or as the Greeks called it "The Dog Days".
Ever since I was a little kid, whenever I felt happy, I felt a little bit scared. I felt like it was going to be this fleeing moment of happiness that is eventually going to escape me and I will be either bored, sad or just neutral.

One summer I decided this shouldn't be the case. I decided to embrace emotions as they should be, those moments of happiness were just about being happy, no mixups; I wanted to make sure I fully experience them, to let the emotion sink in and somehow to be totally surrounded by it.

I realized that I am the happiest version of myself when I am curious, when I am asking questions, when I am learning something new. This is why I decided to embrace my quest for enhancing education by learning about how can I use human-centric design and interactive technology for doing and impact on the society, questioning everything I see along the way and trying to understand humanity in its rawest forms.

MENA Entrepreneurial Eco System Gallery

Rise Up Interactive Technology Gallery
See More Details


The MENA Eco System Gallery is an interactive Gallery where visitors step on a pressure sensor embedded on a step to automatically start a video that provides them with data on the entrepreneurial ecosystem in the corresponding country.

It was deisgned to be a silent space for visiotrs to contemplate the growth of the market and invesmtents in the region within the last 5 years- post Arab Spring. Their were multiple essential elements in the design including: the data, the data representation, the interactive design, the aesthetic layout. The data collected was designed in a way to tell the story of the state being studied. The data was presented in the format of a video in the RU color palette. It was designed to be projected directly from the ceiling as a light beam in the middle of dark areas in between - highlighting the turmoil in other areas in the region.

The Cinematic Orchestra

Embedded Systems Project
See More Details

The Cinematic Orchestra is our final project for the embedded systems course. This course was a competition based course where final project were presented to a panel of professors, industrial gurus from Valeo, Microsoft Research Labs and Amazon to assess the quality of the projects. Our project won First Place. The project is composed of two-folds. First, a drone is set to autonomously fly following a specific color of interest, in our case it was the green ball - as shown in the video. The irobot Roomba then follows this drone through a synchronization communication module between them. The Roomba carries a Kinect Camera that maps the movement of the person carrying the green ball and being followed. It then connects to an animation that is projected through a portable projector. This animated character mimics the movement of the person being followed as shown in the second half of the video.

Midas - Gesture Detection System

CSCE Graduation Thesis
See More Details

Midas is an open source gesture detection library integrable with any app in order to remotely control your machine. Human Computer Interaction (HCI) is an evolving area of research. It develops new ways through which people interact with computers in an easy and more natural way. Throughout the history of computing, people have been dependent on devices through which they could interact with computers. A new trend is the vision-based interaction methods, like hand gestures. Midas uses a depth camera to detect human gestures, recognize them, and trigger actions based on the type of gesture.

Robox - Simaltaneous Localization and Mapping System -

EENG Graduation Thesis
See More Details


Robox is an explorative Robot that we built as part of our Electronics Engineering Major Graduation Project. It carries out simaltaneous localization and mapping in an attempt autonomously explore uninhibited areas. The robot enters unexplored areas, and by navigating around the region, it incrementally builds a 2D floor plan of it. This functionality is useful not only for applications that can make direct use of the map, such as construction surveying, reconnaissance missions, and search and rescue operations, but it is also a fundamental building block for any autonomous robotic system, which must always maintain a model of its environment to be able to interact with it autonomously.
